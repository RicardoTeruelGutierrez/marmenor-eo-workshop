§ 03 — Validation

Does the satellite agree
with the water?

We co-locate each Sentinel-2 sampling point with its nearest buoy (within 4 km, same day) and score the retrieval the way an ocean-colour paper would.

The metrics that matter

  • R² — variance explained
  • RMSE / RMSLE — error (log form, because chl-a is log-normal)
  • Bias — systematic over/under-estimate
  • A 1:1 line on the scatter — the honest visual test

Two cross-validations,
two different questions.

SchemeQuestion it answersResult
TimeSeriesSplitCan we predict future dates? (honours time order)high R²
GroupKFold by stationCan we predict an unseen station? (spatial transfer)R² ≈ 0.17

The gap is the lesson: a model that looks great in time can be weak in space. Report the harder number. A gradient-boosting regressor on reflectances + band ratios + season is the workhorse.

§ 05 — Multivariate anomaly detection

An early-warning detector that
never peeks at the future.

# Fit ONLY on quiet years 2017-18
train = sm.query("date < '2019-01-01'")
iso = IsolationForest(
    n_estimators=300,
    contamination=0.03,
    random_state=42)
iso.fit(train[FEATURES])

# Apply out-of-sample (2019+)
sm["anomaly"] = iso.predict(
    sm[FEATURES])

Why train on quiet years only

If you fit on the whole series, the detector learns the crises as "normal" and its early-warning value evaporates. Fitting on 2017–2018 and applying to 2019+ makes every later flag genuinely out-of-sample.

It watches chl-a, turbidity, DO, SST, salinity and nitrate jointly — joint anomalies catch precursors a single threshold misses.

§ 06 — The metric agencies actually want

Lead time ≠ detection delay.

CrisisFirst flagOutcome
2019 DANA−42 dgenuine early warning
2021 anoxia+2 dcaught after onset (a miss)
2025 bloom+1 dcaught after onset (a miss)

We separate a flag before the start (lead time — the win) from a flag after (detection delay — a miss). Lumping them together is how monitoring papers quietly oversell themselves.

§ 07 — What to walk away with

Module 2 in four sentences.

1Validate before you model. Match-ups with R²/RMSE/bias and a 1:1 line keep you honest.
2Time CV and space CV answer different questions. Report the unseen-station score, not just the easy one.
3Early-warning detectors must train on quiet years and be judged out-of-sample.
4Lead time and detection delay are opposites. Only a flag before onset is a real warning.
