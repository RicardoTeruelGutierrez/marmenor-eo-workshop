§ 01 — Why this lagoon

A small body of water that has
broken three times in a decade.

The Mar Menor is the largest hypersaline coastal lagoon in Europe and one of the most heavily monitored. Decades of nitrate loading from intensive agriculture in the Campo de Cartagena meet sporadic DANA flood events, episodic mining-sediment inputs, and rising Mediterranean SST. The lagoon collapses, recovers and collapses again — and every collapse is visible from orbit at 10-metre resolution.

What it offers as a teaching case

  • Long Sentinel-2 archive (2015–present) at the right resolution
  • Daily Sentinel-3 SLSTR SST since 2017
  • A public, daily in-situ network (CARM / IMIDA)
  • Watershed hydrology from CHS-SAIH
  • Optically Case-2 waters — forces honest engagement with atmospheric correction
  • Strong, dated ground-truth events for skill assessment

§ 02 — The crises, dated

What we will recover from the data.

2016First eutrophication crisis. "Sopa verde" event. Chlorophyll-a reaches 5–6 mg/m³ across the lagoon; Secchi transparency collapses from 5 m to <1 m.
2019DANA flood pulse, September. 300+ mm in 48 h over the Campo de Cartagena. Salinity drops 4 PSU, nitrate spikes ten-fold, three tonnes of dead fish on the shoreline.
2021August anoxia and mass fish-kill. Dissolved oxygen at the surface crashes below 2 mg/L; stratification + marine heatwave + chronic nutrient load.
2025Bloom resurgence. Chl-a anomalies return; regional governance debate intensifies. A renewed test for early-warning systems.

§ 03 — The four data streams

What we extract, and from where.

Sentinel-2 MSI / L2A

10 m, 5-day revisit. Chl-a + turbidity in Case-2 waters via C2RCC atmospheric correction + empirical band ratios. Source: Copernicus Data Space Ecosystem.

Sentinel-3 SLSTR / OLCI

1 km daily SST (SL_2_WST), 300 m OLCI for ocean colour. For lagoon-mean trends and marine-heatwave detection.

In-situ buoys

Daily T, S, chl-a, turbidity, DO, nitrate from the CARM / IMIDA monitoring network. Used for validation and match-up statistics.

Watershed forcing

Precipitation + nitrate-flux proxy from CHS-SAIH (Segura). Connects the lagoon's optical signal back to the agricultural pressure that drives it.

Design principles

  • Lazy I/O. Use xarray + dask to defer computation until needed.
  • STAC over GUI. Discoverable, scriptable, reproducible.
  • Statistical API over scene download. Don't move pixels you can summarize server-side.
  • Time-aware CV. Never random k-fold on EO data; always TimeSeriesSplit or block holdout.
  • Honest baselines. Start with a band ratio before invoking a neural net.

§ 06 — A framing question for the day

"Earth observation can shorten the loop from signal to decision. The slow variable is the politics."

Every algorithm in this workshop already exists in someone's production pipeline somewhere in Murcia. The lagoon still degrades. Hold that tension in mind as we work — what is the role of the scientist when the bottleneck is downstream of the inference?
