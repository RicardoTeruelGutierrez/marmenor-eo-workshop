§ 01 — Why the lagoon is hard

Case-2 waters break the
textbook ocean-colour algorithms.

Open-ocean (Case-1) algorithms assume phytoplankton alone drives the colour. The Mar Menor is shallow, wind-stirred and nutrient-loaded, so three things confound the signal at once.

The operational fix is the C2RCC neural-network atmospheric correction, then a locally-calibrated index. Caveat we state explicitly: Sentinel-2 L2A surface reflectance is a land correction — it is not, by itself, an aquatic C2RCC product.

The three confounders

  • Suspended sediment raises blue/green reflectance → chl-a over-estimated
  • CDOM (coloured dissolved organics from runoff) absorbs in the blue
  • Macrophytes on the bottom add a near-infrared signal

Our chlorophyll proxy

Chl-a ∝ Rrs(B05) / Rrs(B04) — chlorophyll absorbs in the red (665 nm) and reflects in the red-edge (705 nm), so the ratio rises with concentration.

§ 04 — Section A · the kill mechanism

A marine heatwave that
strangles the oxygen.

August 2021 ran ~+2 °C above the climatology. We show the SST field clipped to the real lagoon outline, its anomaly, and the physics that links them to the fish-kill.

30.5°Aug 2021 mean
+1.9°anomaly
−0.3mg/L O₂ lost

The chain, quantified

Warmer water holds less dissolved oxygen (Benson–Krause solubility). The jump from 27.5 °C to 30.5 °C alone removes ~0.3 mg/L of O₂ capacity — and at night the bloom consumes oxygen. Combined with stratification, that is the recipe for bottom anoxia.

Teaching point: SST is not a curiosity — it is a direct, computable driver of the ecological collapse.

What students must remember

  • SciHub is dead; CDSE is the entry point.
  • New STAC root stac.dataspace.copernicus.eu/v1; collection is lowercase sentinel-2-l2a.
  • Use L2A (surface), not L1C (top-of-atmosphere).
  • The COG trick: read only the AOI window — a few MB, not the ~800 MB scene.
